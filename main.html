Sarah for Freedom

Advocating for Informed Consent, Free Speech, & Constitutional Freedoms

About Sarah

Sarah is a former Registered Practical Nurse (RPN) from Ontario, Canada, with over a decade of experience caring for children with disabilities, senior residents in long-term care, and advocating as a union leader. She became a vocal critic of certain COVID-19 policies, believing that lockdowns and mandates were causing more harm than good. After speaking publicly and challenging the official narrative, she faced backlash, lost her nursing positions, and is now in a legal battle to defend her right—and the right of all healthcare professionals—to speak freely.

Emergencies Act Challenge

In early 2022, Sarah and other members of the group Canadian Frontline Nurses joined the peaceful Freedom Convoy protests in Ottawa. Prime Minister Trudeau invoked the Emergencies Act, allowing banks to freeze protestors’ accounts without due process. Believing this was an unconstitutional use of power designed to silence lawful dissent, Sarah’s group became one of the first to challenge the invocation in Federal Court. The fight continues on appeal, where Sarah seeks to reaffirm Canadians’ fundamental freedoms and expose the political motives behind the government’s extreme measures.

College of Nurses Discipline Case

The College of Nurses of Ontario (CNO) is attempting to discipline Sarah over social media posts and a public speech that questioned public health guidelines. The CNO alleges her information was “false, misleading, or inaccurate.” Sarah argues that her views are backed by research and expert testimony—testimony the College has refused to hear. She is now awaiting the outcome of a lengthy hearing and is prepared to appeal any decision that undermines a nurse’s right to engage in open scientific dialogue and free expression.
